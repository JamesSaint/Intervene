Services

Three services. One question.

Each service tests a different dimension of the same question: when a system fails, can the right person act in time? Every engagement begins where that question becomes impossible to avoid.

01How we work

Scoped in writing. Practitioner-led. Independent of any vendor, framework, or advisory network.

Findings are accurate, not reassuring. Every assessment uses a proprietary method designed to test whether intervention is actually possible under the conditions your organisation would face.

  • A confidential discussion to establish context and fit.
  • Written scope before any work begins.
  • Senior advisors with direct operational experience.
  • Findings delivered directly to the board or executive team.

02The services
01

Exposure Assessment

One system. One scenario. A clear view of where intervention breaks.

Who it is for
  • Boards and executives accountable for a specific consequential system
  • CROs, CTOs, and senior risk owners in regulated environments
  • Leaders aware that governance exists but uncertain whether it produces action
What it reveals
  • Whether signal reaches the right people in time
  • Whether escalation can move at the speed the situation demands
  • Whether authority is clear enough to act
  • Where the chain breaks before a decision is possible
What you receive
  • Exposure map anchored to the specific system and scenario
  • Intervention viability report
  • Prioritised remediation recommendations
  • Board-ready executive summary

Why it matters: a board that cannot see where the chain breaks cannot govern what it cannot see.

02

Board-Level Advisory

Independent judgement in high-stakes decisions where internal certainty may be misleading.

Who it is for
  • Boards facing decisions where governance documents are not sufficient
  • Executives navigating vendor dependencies or AI deployment at scale
  • Risk committees operating inside organisations that feel in control
What it reveals
  • Where organisational confidence is not supported by operational reality
  • Whether the current decision architecture can survive contact with the specific scenario at hand
  • Structural assumptions that would break under pressure
What you receive
  • Independent judgement at the moment it is needed
  • Confidential written findings and options
  • Direct engagement with the decision owner
  • Post-decision audit trail where required

Why it matters: the most dangerous failures are the ones the organisation cannot see from the inside.

03

Ongoing Integrity Oversight

Continued visibility across critical systems, decision structures, and intervention capability.

Who it is for
  • Organisations running multiple consequential systems
  • Boards that want a standing independent view of intervention capability
  • Leaders building durable oversight as systems and exposure evolve
What it reveals
  • How intervention capability is changing as systems and dependencies evolve
  • Where new exposure is emerging before it becomes failure
  • Whether remediation is producing real change, not documentation
What you receive
  • Quarterly integrity reporting to the board
  • Independent reassessment as systems change
  • Escalation route for live concerns
  • Continuity of senior advisor access

Why it matters: intervention capability decays the moment it stops being tested.

03Scope and engagement

Written scope

Every engagement is defined in writing before work begins. Deliverables, timelines, and confidentiality are agreed up front. No informal advisory outside a scoped engagement.

Limited engagements

Intervene works with a deliberately small number of organisations at any one time. Each engagement receives the full attention of senior advisors.

Independence

Intervene is not affiliated with any technology vendor, governance body, or advisory network. Findings are not shaped by the interests that commission most advisory work.

Confidentiality

All conversations are treated as confidential from first contact. No client information or findings are shared without explicit written consent.
