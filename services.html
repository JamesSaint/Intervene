§ 03 · Engagement shapes

Four delivery shapes. Each one self-contained.

Every engagement publishes a written scope before work begins. No informal advisory; no scope creep; no implementation. Findings, not delivery.

01 · Tier 1

Exposure Assessment.

One system. One scenario. A clear view of where intervention capability breaks down, anchored against the PRA's evidence bar.

WhoBoards and executives accountable for a specific material model or operational system.
RevealsThe chain break. Whether governance translates to operational control. Where the evidence ceiling caps your stated capability.
ReceivesFailure Exposure Report, CRR scoring, regulatory mapping, board presentation, signed envelope.

A board that cannot see where the chain breaks cannot govern what it cannot see.

From £120,000 · 8–10 weeks
02 · Held

Board-Level Advisory.

Independent judgement in high-stakes decisions where internal certainty may be misleading. Vendor dependencies, scaled AI deployment, irreversible commitments.

WhoBoards navigating decisions where governance documents are not sufficient and internal confidence is structurally suspect.
RevealsThe framing the organisation cannot see from the inside. The exposure that internal advocates have a reason not to surface.
ReceivesDirect judgement, in writing, on a defined decision. Off record unless agreed otherwise.

The most dangerous failures are the ones the organisation cannot see from the inside.

By application
03 · Held

Ongoing Integrity Oversight.

Continued visibility across critical systems, decision structures, and intervention capability. For organisations running multiple consequential systems.

WhoOrganisations that have completed a Tier 1 baseline and want a standing independent view as exposure evolves.
RevealsDrift, decay, and the silent failure of controls that were once tested and have since stopped being.
ReceivesQuarterly attested re-scoring, drift register, escalation memos when a control crosses a threshold.

Intervention capability decays the moment it stops being tested.

On application · post Tier 1
04 · Tier 2–4

Engine Licensing.

For groups that want the engine in-house. AGDA, the rater protocol, the calibration discipline, and the signing infrastructure — operated under licence, with quarterly methodology updates.

WhoRegulated groups conducting multiple internal assessments per year, or assurance functions standardising on a deterministic engine.
RevealsComparable, attested results across business lines. No internal-rater drift. A defensible baseline.
ReceivesAnnual licence, rater certification, signing keys, quarterly engine and corpus updates.

A signed computation is the same finding twice. Two opinions are not.

£45k–£275k / year
§ 04 · Engagement model

How we work. Written. Bounded. Insurable.

The commercial frame is identical across tiers. Master Services Agreement governs every engagement. Liability capped at fees paid. Five-year confidentiality. No exceptions.

M · 01
Written scope before any work.

No verbal scoping. No "preliminary thinking" in advance of agreement. Master Services Agreement, fees, deliverables, and exclusions are signed before evidence is requested.

M · 02
Independence by design.

No vendor affiliation. No referral fees. No co-marketing with assessed parties. Framework mappings are auditable; we publish what we cite.

M · 03
Limited concurrency.

Small team by intent. We will turn down engagements that exceed our reviewable capacity. Quality is bounded by the rater pool, not by sales pipeline.

M · 04
Insured. Indemnified.

Professional indemnity £5M E&O cover. Liability capped at fees paid for the engagement. Confidentiality binds both parties for five years from the close of work.
