About / Overview — / 2000
Intervene is an independent advisory practice for boards and executives accountable for systems where the gap between governance design and operational reality carries real consequence. We answer one question: can the people responsible for your most consequential system actually stop it when they need to? Most assessments measure intention. Intervene measures evidence. Our methodology — AGDA™ — pairs a published, falsifiable framework with a deterministic engine that signs every verdict it produces. Eight falsifiable claims. Forty canonical failure modes. Ed25519 cryptographic attestation. A regulator or audit committee can verify a finding without contacting us. We work with three audiences: — UK-regulated financial services (PRA SS1/23, FCA SMCR, operational resilience) — EU high-risk AI operators (EU AI Act Annex III, conformity, post-market monitoring) — Critical national infrastructure (energy, water, telecoms, transport) Healthcare and defence by invitation, where independence from device vendors and appropriate clearances can be guaranteed. Three services. One question. 01 · Exposure Assessment — One system. One scenario. A clear view of where intervention capability breaks down. 02 · Board-Level Advisory — Independent judgement in high-stakes decisions where internal certainty may be misleading. 03 · Ongoing Integrity Oversight — Standing visibility across critical systems, decision structures, and intervention capability as exposure evolves. What we are not: not a compliance dashboard, not a Big-4 advisory, not a policy-writing practice, not a model-testing lab, not an implementation partner. We do not implement what we recommend. We do not assess what we have helped to design. Senior delivery throughout. A signed verdict, not a slide. Intervene Group Ltd · Registered in England & Wales · London · Global mandates. About (max 2,600 chars) — / 2600
I founded Intervene to address a question most governance frameworks leave untested: can the people accountable for a consequential system actually stop it when they need to? The crisis begins the moment a system fails. Authority to halt it travels upward through people who often cannot act. By the time it reaches a board, the moment for low-cost intervention has frequently passed. A board that cannot intervene is not a safeguard. It is a witness. Intervene exists to close that gap before it costs you the room to act. WHAT I DO I lead independent intervention-capability assessments for boards and executives in regulated and high-stakes environments. The methodology — AGDA — is published, falsifiable, and revision-controlled. The engine that produces verdicts is deterministic and Ed25519-signed: same inputs, same outputs, every time. An audit committee or regulator can verify a finding without contacting us. WHO I WORK WITH — UK-regulated financial services (PRA SS1/23, FCA SMCR, operational resilience) — EU high-risk AI operators (EU AI Act Annex III) — Critical national infrastructure (energy, water, telecoms, transport) — Healthcare and defence, by invitation only BACKGROUND Two decades building, deploying, and reviewing the kinds of consequential systems that reach a board only when something has gone wrong: regulated AI deployment, decision-system design, and board-level oversight across UK and EU markets. The methodology behind Intervene is developed in academic collaboration with Prof. Nicholas Ryman-Tubb (University of Surrey), with calibration drawn from a hold-out corpus of regulated AI failure modes. WHAT I DON'T DO I do not write your AI policy. I do not stand up your controls. I do not implement what I recommend, and I do not assess what I have helped design. No leveraged engagement teams. No PowerPoint readouts. Senior delivery throughout — a signed verdict, not a slide. If the evidence does not support a green rating, the engine will not produce one — regardless of pressure. I will return findings the client did not want to hear, in writing, before the regulator does. WHEN TO REACH OUT If you are a chair, NED, CEO, CRO, CISO, GC, or Head of AI/Risk and you are not certain your organisation could stop its most consequential system right now — that is worth a conversation. Brief, confidential, no obligation either side. advisory@intervene.group · intervene.group Based in London. Global mandates.
