Method · 2026

A deterministic engine, not a methodology deck.

AGDA™ scores intervention capability against evidence, not against intention. Published methodology. Cryptographically attested output. The calculations that matter, made legible.

§ 01 · The Assertion

Governance is a timed causal chain, not a portfolio of artefacts.

You cannot escalate what you did not detect. You cannot decide on what was not escalated. You cannot intervene without a decision. The chain is ordered because causation is ordered. Each stage is necessary. None is sufficient.

Stage 1
Signal
Did the problem generate a detectable signal?
Stage 2
Escalation
Did the signal reach an authority with decision rights?
Stage 3
Decision
Was a decision to intervene made?
Stage 4
Intervention
Did the action take effect before harm became irreversible?

A chain's capability equals its weakest stage. Averaging dimensions into a composite score is marketing. The chain is the control.

§ 02 · Eight Dimensions

The four chain stages, plus the four conditions that determine whether each stage holds.

AGDA scores every assessed control along eight dimensions. The first four map to the chain stages. The remaining four are the operational conditions that determine whether control is real or inherited from a document that has not been stress-tested.

D1

Signal

Does the system surface the conditions that require human attention, clearly and in time?

D2

Escalation

Does a signal reach a person with authority to act before the window to act has closed?

D3

Decision

Can the decision be made under pressure, with incomplete information and real constraints?

D4

Intervention

Can action be taken at the moment it matters, not after the consequence has compounded?

D5

Accountability

Is responsibility assigned in practice, not only on paper? Does it survive organisational change?

D6

Human oversight

Is oversight informed, authorised, and capable of altering outcomes? Or is it ceremonial?

D7

Auditability

Is the decision trail complete, reliable, and defensible to the people who will later ask what happened?

D8

Capacity

Are the people, authority, and resources required to act actually present when the moment arrives?

§ 03 · Evidence-Grade Capping

Paper evidence caps at amber. You cannot reach green on a policy alone.

Most scoring systems treat documented intention and stress-tested capability as comparable. They are not. AGDA enforces an evidence-grade ceiling on every claim — applied before weighting. The lowest-evidence claim anchors the dimension. This is the single rule competitors cannot match without rebuilding their engine.

Grade 0
Assumed

We say we can do it. No record exists.

Caps at 1 / 5
Grade 1
Documented in policy

The policy describes the capability. It has not been tested.

Caps at 2 / 5
Grade 2
Tested in drill

A tabletop or drill has exercised the capability under controlled conditions.

Caps at 3 / 5
Grade 3
Operational in production

The capability is running in production against real events.

Caps at 4 / 5
Grade 4
Stress-validated under realistic pressure

The capability has worked against the specific stressors it is designed for.

Clears to 5 / 5

Why it matters. A client with a perfect compliance-dashboard score can fail an AGDA assessment. That is the product, not a bug. Until the control has actually worked under real pressure, its capability cannot be rated as if it had.

§ 04 · Chain-Ceiling Propagation

A downstream stage cannot score higher than upstream, plus one.

If Detection scores 2, Intervention cannot be above 3 — regardless of what the intervention questions claim. The rule is not a heuristic; it is operational topology. You cannot intervene on what you never detected. Claimed downstream strength is latent, untestable capability.

Signal
2.0
Claimed: 4.0
Escalation
3.0 ← capped
Claimed: 4.5
Decision
3.5 ← capped
Claimed: 4.8
Intervention
4.0 ← capped

Example. A bank claims intervention capability of 4.8 on a material credit model. Detection scores 2.0 because signal is limited to nightly batch review. The chain ceiling propagates the weakness forward: intervention is capped at 3.0 + 1.0 = 4.0. Claimed 4.8 is structural fiction. Submitting this to the PRA as evidence of §6 compliance would misrepresent the actual capability.

§ 05 · Integrity Stance

Four positions the engine takes. Non-negotiable.

These are the structural rules the engine enforces, in code, on every assessment. They are what distinguishes measurement-grade assessment from narrative assessment.

01

Coincidence is not a margin.

Absence of incidents is evidence of untested exposure, not of effective controls. Two years without incident may reflect low traffic, favourable conditions, or vigilant operators about to leave. A control's score reflects what it can do under stress, not what has not yet been required of it.

02

Strong accountability cannot mask weak oversight.

Naming an SMF4 is a partial control. Pairing that designation with oversight authority limited to "challenge rights" is governance theatre — the accountable person cannot halt the thing they are accountable for. The engine reads the pair together, and penalises the contradiction.

03

Paper evidence caps at amber.

Policy without drill maxes at red. Drill without production maxes at amber. Production without stress validation maxes below green. Green requires evidence that the control has actually worked under real conditions. This is deliberately strict. A tool that let policy-only controls score green would be measuring intention, not capability.

04

Confidence must not exceed evidence.

Organisations routinely express high confidence in controls whose evidence grade is low. The engine quantifies and penalises this — the Delusion Gap Index. Stated confidence exceeding evidence by 2+ points on a 5-point scale demotes the dimension. Overconfidence in weakly-evidenced controls is a governance failure, not an emotional state.

§ 06 · Attestation

Every verdict is cryptographically signed. The regulator can verify without us.

Big-4 advisories produce PDFs. GRC platforms produce dashboards. Neither is reproducible; neither is independently verifiable. AGDA produces a deterministic signed computation — the same inputs produce the same outputs, every time, bound to a specific engine version, with a signature verifiable using a committed public key.

§ 07 · Engagement Shape

Three stages. Each one ships a written output before the next begins.

No stage proceeds without written agreement on scope and outcome. No informal advisory. No scope creep. The engagement shape is identical across Tier 0, Tier 1, and licensed assessments — only the depth and attestation surface change.

Stage 1

Exposure

We identify the system, the decision context, the risk scenario, and the assumed intervention path.

  • Written scope and decision map
  • System, scenario, and authority chain documented
  • Assumed intervention path, recorded as stated
  • Evidence request list issued
Stage 2

Reality Testing

We test whether signal, escalation, decision, and intervention can actually happen — under time pressure and dependency constraints.

  • SEDI dimension scoring against evidence grade
  • Chain-ceiling and contradiction analysis
  • Temporal gate evaluation against reversibility window
  • CRR portfolio classification per control
Stage 3

Decision Support

Findings are prioritised, specific, and delivered directly to the Board or executive team.

  • Attested Failure Exposure Report issued
  • Regulatory mapping register finalised
  • Direct presentation to decision owner
  • Prioritised remediation, not implemented by us
§ 08 · Published Methodology

Citeable. Falsifiable.

The methodology is public. The calibration is not. This is standard trade-secret practice: publish the interface, protect the implementation. A reader can evaluate the thinking, cite it in regulatory correspondence, and criticise the claims. Revisions are recorded; claims have revision numbers.
