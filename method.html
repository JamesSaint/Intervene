Method

How we see what others miss

Most governance reviews ask whether a framework exists. We test whether it works. A proprietary assessment engine, a defined engagement process, and findings written for decision-makers.

01The assessment engine

InterveneCore™ is how we structure what we find.

Not a product. Not a platform. A proprietary method for determining whether an organisation can intervene in time, with the right authority, on evidence it can trust.

It turns the question "are we in control?" into something testable. The answer is often uncomfortable. It is always specific.

  • Operated by senior Intervene advisors, not licensed to clients.
  • Applied to real systems and real authority structures, not theoretical scenarios.
  • Calibrated to operational reality, not documented intent.
  • Produces findings a board can act on, not reassurance it can file.
02Eight dimensions of control

Every assessment tests eight dimensions. These are the conditions that determine whether control is real or inherited from a document no one has stress-tested.

01

Signal

Does the system surface the conditions that require human attention, clearly and in time?

02

Escalation

Does a signal reach the person with authority to act before the window to act has closed?

03

Decision

Can the decision be made under pressure, with incomplete information and real constraints?

04

Intervention

Can action be taken at the moment it matters, not after the consequence has compounded?

05

Accountability

Is responsibility assigned in practice, not only on paper? Does it survive organisational change?

06

Human oversight

Is oversight informed, authorised, and capable of altering outcomes? Or is it ceremonial?

07

Auditability

Is the decision trail complete, reliable, and defensible to the people who will later ask what happened?

08

Capacity

Are the people, authority, and resources required to act actually present when the moment arrives?

03Three stages

Every engagement follows the same structure. Each stage produces a clear output before the next begins. No stage proceeds without written agreement on scope and outcome.

01
Exposure
We identify the system, the decision context, the risk scenario, and the assumed intervention path. The purpose is to establish what the organisation believes to be true and where that belief is untested.

Reality Testing

  • Direct presentation to the decision owner.
04Scope and confidentiality

Every engagement is scoped in writing before work begins. The scope defines what will be assessed, what it will produce, and the boundaries of the advisory relationship. There is no informal work, no unscoped access, and no drift.

Confidentiality is structural. Findings go to named decision-makers and no one else. Intervene does not publish case studies, name clients, or discuss engagements outside the room.

  • Written scope before any work begins.
  • Named decision-makers. Nothing shared beyond them.
  • Independent. No vendor, framework, or advisory network alignment.
  • Clean exit. Each engagement ends with a defined handover.
